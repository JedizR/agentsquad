# Business Consultant — Research Framework Reference

## How to Structure Research Queries

### The 5-Question Framework
Before starting any research, define:
1. **The specific question** — not "tell me about the market" but "what is the average monthly churn rate for B2B SaaS in the $49-$99/mo price range?"
2. **The time horizon** — are we looking for current data (2025–2026) or historical trends?
3. **The geography** — global, North America, Southeast Asia?
4. **The customer segment** — SMB, enterprise, indie developers, agencies?
5. **The confidence threshold** — is a blog post acceptable, or do we need a primary source?

### Query Templates

**Competitor analysis:**
```
Search for: [product name] OR "[product category]" pricing site:[competitor.com]
OR "[product category] competitors 2026" site:g2.com OR site:capterra.com
Find: pricing tiers, feature comparisons, user reviews, market position
```

**Market sizing:**
```
Search for: "[market name] market size 2025 2026" site:statista.com OR site:grandviewresearch.com OR site:marketsandmarkets.com
Find: TAM, SAM, growth rate, key segments
Caveat: market research firms often over-estimate — cross-reference with 2+ sources
```

**Pricing benchmarks:**
```
Search for: "SaaS pricing" [category] 2026 per seat OR per user OR flat rate
Find: real product pricing pages, not articles about pricing
Source: product websites directly > analysis articles > blog posts
```

## Source Quality Hierarchy
1. Primary source (company's own website, SEC filing, official data release) — Confidence: High
2. Tier-1 journalism (TechCrunch, Bloomberg, Reuters, WSJ) — Confidence: High
3. Research firm (Statista, Gartner, Forrester — note: often behind paywall) — Confidence: Medium-High
4. Review site (G2, Capterra, Trustpilot) — Confidence: Medium (user-reported)
5. Blog post / opinion article — Confidence: Low (use only for directional guidance)

## When Data is Unavailable
- State explicitly: "No reliable data found for [X]. The following is inferred from adjacent data: [Y]."
- Never fabricate a statistic
- A "I could not find reliable data" answer is MORE valuable than a confident wrong answer
